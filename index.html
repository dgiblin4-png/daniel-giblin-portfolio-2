About Me

A broad foundation in research, writing, and analysis.

I am a History and Policy student at the University of Maryland with a strong foundation in research, analysis, and written communication. My academic work has trained me to examine complex issues carefully, evaluate evidence, and turn detailed material into clear, organized arguments.

Across history, policy, and media-related work, I have developed a consistent approach: understand the system, identify the most important patterns, and communicate insights in a way that is both precise and accessible. I value thoughtful interpretation, attention to detail, and work that is grounded in evidence.

That combination of research discipline, critical thinking, and communication gives me a flexible professional foundation across a range of paths, including research, communications, strategy, policy, and other roles where analytical judgment matters.

02

Race, Gender, Masculinity, and Combat in WWII

Examined how race, gender, and masculinity shaped combat, military identity, and wartime experience during World War II.

Historical research Critical thinking Cultural analysis
View Project

Overview

This paper explores the ways wartime ideas about race, gender, and masculinity shaped both combat roles and the meaning attached to military service in World War II. It combines historical interpretation with cultural analysis to examine how identity and power influenced wartime experience.

What I Did

  • Researched historical sources related to combat, identity, and wartime culture.
  • Analyzed how race and gender expectations shaped military narratives and experience.
  • Built a written argument that connected social identity to the realities of war.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Historical research and interpretation
  • Critical thinking
  • Synthesis of complex cultural and historical ideas

Why It Matters

The project shows an ability to analyze how broader social systems influence institutions and lived experience, then communicate that analysis through clear academic writing.

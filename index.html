About Me

A broad foundation in research, writing, and analysis.

I am a History and Policy student at the University of Maryland with a strong foundation in research, analysis, and written communication. My academic work has trained me to examine complex issues carefully, evaluate evidence, and turn detailed material into clear, organized arguments.

Across history, policy, media, and infrastructure-related work, I have developed a consistent approach: understand the system, identify the most important patterns, and communicate insights in a way that is both precise and accessible. I value thoughtful interpretation, attention to detail, and work grounded in evidence.

That combination of research discipline, critical thinking, and communication gives me a flexible professional foundation across a range of paths, including research, communications, strategy, policy, infrastructure, sustainability, and other roles where analytical judgment matters.

LSATSwipe is a self-conceived practice tool that turns LSAT Logical Reasoning prep into a fast, swipe-based experience. I built it after diagnosing my own pacing and accuracy gaps on the LSAT and deciding a better practice tool was the more useful thing to build than another study guide.
